REGULATIONS FOR CONDUCTING SHIP
CHANDLER SERVICES IN THE HASHEMITE
KINGDOM OF JORDAN
Issued by the Board of Directors of Jordan Maritime Commission by virtue of
Paragraph (k) of Article 9 of Jordan Maritime Commission Law No. (47) for the
year 2002
Article (1)
These regulations shall be named (REGULATIONS FOR CONDUCTING SHIP
CHANDLERING SERVICES IN THE HASHEMITE KINGDOM OF JORDAN for
the year 2003) and shall come into force as of the date of publication in the
Official Gazette.

Article (3)
It is permitted to practice vessels supply services in the Hashemite Kingdom
of Jordan through a company or an organization registered and licensed by
the following authorities:
a) Jordan Maritime Commission if the ship chandler headquarters is
outside the borderlines of Aqaba Special Economic Zone Authority
(ASEZA) under the provisions of these Regulations.

b) Aqaba Special Economic Zone Authority (ASEZA) in coordination
with the Commission if its headquarters is within the borderlines of
ASEZ in accordance with ASEZA Law No. (32) of 2000 and the
regulations and instructions issued pursuant thereto.
Article (4)
The Director General shall form a committee of the Commission's staff to do
the following:
a) Study the papers and documents submitted for the purpose of
licensing. The Commission may request to be provided with any
papers or documents deemed necessary for approving the license.
b) Check the companies and organizations practicing vessels supply
services and follow up on their compliance with the licensing
conditions and the provisions of these Regulations.
Article (5)
The ship chandler services provided to ships calling at the Port of Aqaba
shall be defined as follows:
a) Provide vessels with foodstuffs, medical supplies, consumables, work
clothes and personal safety equipment.
b) Provide vessels with navigational equipment, supplies, navigational
maps, office materials, cleaning materials, and any materials
necessary to sustain the operation of vessels except electrical and
mechanical spare parts, electric and electronic switchboards, pumps,
generators, mineral oils, chemicals, industrial gases and refrigerants
used in engine rooms for vessels.
c) Provide cleaning services, rations and blankets for ship crews
Article (6)
Ship chandler services shall be conducted through an independent license,
separate from any other maritime services, including, ship surveys and
representation of protection or freight forwarding services, except for marine
maintenance services.
Article (7)
Ship chandlers shall undertake to supply the ship with the material in
accordance with the requirements of quality and public health in accordance

with the legislation of Jordan in force and to submit a copy of the order for
the delivery of the material supplied to the ship master to the Commission
and the general manager of the ship chandler on the stationary of the ship
chandler.
Article (8)
The conditions for granting a license to practice vessels supply shall be as
follows:
a) The ship chandler has to be registered in the Kingdom as a company
or organization at the Companies Controller Department at the
Ministry of Industry and Trade.
b) The company's paid-up and registered capital must not be less than
(10,000) ten thousand Jordanian Dinars.
c) The company shall have a full time general manager holding the
Jordanian Nationality.
d) The ship chandler employees must not be less than (3) including the
General Manager.
e) The ship chandler must practice his activities at an independent office
of an area not less than (50) square meters, equipped with a telephone,
fax and computer connected to the internet.
Article (9)
The procedures for granting a license to practice vessels supply services
outside the borderlines of Aqaba Special Economic Zone (ASEZA) are as
follows:
a) Submit the vessel supply services registration application to the
Ministry of Industry and Trade specifying the objectives of the
company/organization in accordance with Article (5) of these
regulations.
b) The application for registration shall be forwarded to the Commission
for consideration and issuance of the preliminary approval. The
license applicant shall be given the form of the license conditions to
complete the registration procedures. Ihe Commission may send the
license form and issue the initial approval by fax or e-mail.
c) The license applicant shall submit the duly filled license application
form accompanied by the following documents:
1. A certified copy of the Contract of Association stating the
names of partners or the name of the owner of the organization,

the objectives of the company/organization, the certificate of
registration and a certificate showing the commissioners of the
management of the company or organization who are
authorized to sign on the administrative, financial and judicial
matters. It should be duly certified by the Ministry of Industry
and Trade.
2. A certified copy of the title deed or lease contract for the ship
chandler' s offices.
3. A certified copy of the profession license.
4. A copy of the employment contract of each employee working
for the ship chandler certified by the Ministry of Labor and a
certificate issued by the Social Security Corporation including
the names of employees who are under the employer's social
security.
5. The ship chandler's number in the Income Tax Department.
6. Qualifications and experience documents of the Director
General of the ship chandler.
d) The committee formed under these regulations shall check the
location of the ship chandler to ascertain that the requirements of the
conditions stipulated in Article (8) of these regulations are satisfied
and to verify the information contained in the license form.
e) The Committee shall recommend to the Director General to issue a
work license for the ship chandler.
f) The Director General shall issue the work license for the ship chandler
after paying the annual fees prescribed for one year and it shall be
renewed annually by a recommendation from the Committee and the
concerned parties related to company services shall be contacted to
issue the necessary entry permits.
Article (10)
The Authority shall, in the manner it deems appropriate and at any time,
ascertain that the ship chandler complies with the provisions of these
Regulations and the Director General shall suspend the license temporarily
or permanently cancel it in case of violation of the license terms and not
removing the violation after informing the ship chandler of the period that
the Director General deems appropriate to remove the violation.
Article (11)

All entities licensed to carry out ship supply services in the Kingdom shall
comply with these regulations within a period not exceeding six months
from the date of its publication in the Official Gazette.
Article (12)
Act No. (46) for the year 1984 (the system of supplying ships with
foodstuffs) and its amendments shall be suspended and replaced by these
instructions as of the date of their publication in the Official Gazette.
