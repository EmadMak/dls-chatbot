Article (3)
The Minister shall form a committee called (The committee to follow-up the
implementation of these regulations and issue of the official documents,
concerning the Tugboat masters in The Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan)
headed by the Director General of Jordan Maritime Commission with the
membership of:
1- The Director of Ship Registration and Seafarers Affairs Department of
Jordan Maritime Commission or his representative who holds the
qualification of a Chief Engineer or High-Seas Captain.
2- An expert in the field of Tugboats and pilotage who resides in Aqaba City
if possible
Article (4)
Jordanian Territorial waters are demarcated by the position zero at the Red
Sea Northeast coast of Aqaba Gulf, where the marine borderline extends
vertically on the longitude line, which crosses the zero point and extends
toward the west for 2.84 Kilo Meters, where it meets the line dividing Aqaba
Gulf into two halves, and from that point this line tracks the marine
borderline, which divides the Gulf and extends toward the south at the last
point, to be vertical on the borderline of the Saudi Arabia Kingdom, at the
east coast, facing Durrah Border Center.
Article (5)
The Commission, through the committee mentioned in Article (3) of these
regulations, shall undertake the required testing for Tug boat master
licensing and recommend the issuance of the official documents necessary
for granting the license

The conditions for granting the Tugboats master's license to drive Tugboats
at Jordanian Ports and territorial waters are as follows:
1- The applicant must be of a Jordanian Nationality, or contracted with the
competent authority to work as Tugboat master at Jordanian Ports.
2- He must acquire a certificate of competency as a second marine officer at
least.
3- He must not be less than 25 years old.
4- He must not be convicted against a felony or misdemeanor offences that
are immoral or unethical.
5- He must have completed the training requirements specified in Article (8)
of these Regulations
Article (7)
The required documents for the license applications are as follows:
1- A document proving his Jordanian Nationality or a document
proving that he works with the parties who are permitted to
practice Tugboats operations at Jordanian Ports and territorial
waters.
2- A valid second marine officer certificate of competency,
certified by the Commission.
3- A valid health certificate, to prove his health fitness to practice
the work as a Tugboat master.
4- The required mandatory safety certificate for acquiring the
second marine officer certificate of competency.
5- A non-conviction certificate.
6- Mastering the English Language.
Article (8)
The training requirements for granting the Tug boat master driving license
to new Tug boat masters are as follows:
1- Going through a training period of three to nine months in driving
Tugboats and maneuvering operations of ships at the port of Aqaba under
185

the supervision of licensed Tugboat masters. Such training has to be proved
by an experience certificate endorsed by the concerned authorities.
2- The Commission shall keep a special record for each Tug boat master
who has completed the required training recorded down in it all information
pertaining to the ships, terminals and weather conditions through which the
apprentice Tug boat master has performed his driving and towing of Tug
boats, along with the notes of the Tug boat master and any notes regarding
the maneuvers he has participated in, in order to be submitted to the
Committee.
3- The Tug boat master shall be trained on all kinds of marine Tugboats with
a propulsion power of not less than 1400 KW, and conducted at all Aqaba
Port Terminals and during various weather conditions, excluding towing and
berthing ships into and out of the oil terminal and ships carrying highly
dangerous materials.
Article (9)
The procedures for testing the Tug boat master are specified as follows:
1- Submitting a written application to the Commission for testing the new
apprentice Tug boat master.
2- The Committee shall set a date for the test which should be within two
weeks of the date of submitting the application.
3- The testing procedures shall be carried out by using an appropriate Tug
boat.
4- The party who employs the apprentice Tug boat master shall provide the
Tug boat needed for training and testing purposes.
5- The test shall be carried out several times to enable the Committee to be
assured of the Tugboat master competence and ability to drive the Tug boat
alone and properly maneuver to get ships into and out of various terminals
Article (10)
A- Upon passing the required test, the new Tug boat master, recruited for
less than a year of the date of publication of these regulations and have met
the terms and conditions of acquiring the driving license for Tug boats,
would be granted a driving license valid for one year to operate Tug boats
with a power capacity of not less than 1400 KW.

B- The Tug boat master who has acquired the temporary driving license
mentioned in paragraph (A) of this Article, and who has completed a period
of one year after the date of obtaining the temporary license, shall be granted
Tug boat driving license without any limitations to perform various piloting
operations at Jordanian ports, after providing the Commission with a letter
from the body where he worked, proving that he has worked for that body,
and recorded in it the experience hours and efficiency. The Commission may
examine the applicant if necessary.
Article (11)
The Director is entitled to withdraw or stop or cancel the license of any Tug
boat master, in case he forfeits one of the terms of granting him the license
as a Tug boat master or has been caught intoxicated while on duty or has
committed a huge mistake during performing his work leading to an accident
that has caused human injuries, marine pollution or damage to the safety of
navigation after conducting an investigation to the matter.
Article (12)
A- Tug boat masters currently engaged in operations at Jordanian Ports who
are not new, shall be granted a Tug boat master license upon submitting the
documents mentioned in Article (7) of these regulations, without having to
take the training course or the test provided for in these regulations.
B- Tug boat masters, operating at the Jordanian ports, and on whom the
rulings of paragraph (A) of this Article apply, shall adjust their status within
(24) months from the date of publication of these regulations.
Article (13)
The Director General, upon a recommendation from the Committee for
justified reasons, may grant a tugboat master license to operate at the
Jordanian ports, which require special training to work operate in.
Article (14)
All previous Regulations in these matters shall be cancelled as of the date of
publication of these regulations in the Official Gazette
